Why should you use eSLIME?

There are many modeling systems, such as NetLogo, that could potentially meet your needs. eSLIME is designed to run many iterations of your model, very quickly, and often on many different computers. This allows you to make quantitative, rather than descriptive, assessments of your system's behavior. This may be particularly useful for ecologists and biophysicists.

 

What sorts of models can I develop in eSLIME?

eSLIME models can feature individual-based rules ("bottom-up" modeling) and top-down perturbations, such as population rearrangements or system-wide update rules. Models take place on one or more coupled lattices. Positions in the lattice can either have one of a finite number of discrete states or a continuum of states. Most lattice models can therefore be implemented in eSLIME.

 

What does "eSLIME" stand for?

eSLIME stands for "extensible Sequential Lattice Iteration Modeling Engine.”

 

Who can use or modify eSLIME?

eSLIME is offered with a Creative Commons Attribution International 4.0 license. Anyone can download and modify eSLIME, for any purpose (including commercial purposes), as long as they provide proper attribution. In fact, we'd love to hear how you're using eSLIME.
